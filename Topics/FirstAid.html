4 Point plan:

  1. Primary survey (DRSABCD)
  2. Vital sign survey
  3. Secondary survey (Head to toe)
  4. Oxygen, Position, Reassurance

DRSABCDE:

  • Danger: to self, others and patient. What might have caused their state?
  • Response COWS and AVPU
  • Send for help
  • Airway: clear and open airway, recovery position. To open airway: jaw thrust, head tilt
  • Breathing: check for 2 breaths in 10s. Look, listen, feel.
  • Circulation or CPR: check for pulse, control bleeding. For CPR: 30 compressions 2 breaths - use BVM
  • Defibrillation or Disability: apply defibrillator ASAP (if unconscious, not breathing)
  • Environment or Exposure: protect from elements, consider HAZMAT

Response Assessment - COWS and AVPU:

  • Can you see me?
  • Open your eyes
  • What is your name?
  • Squeeze my hand
  • AVPU: Alert, Verbal, Pain responsive, Unresponsive

Normal Vital signs:

  • Pulse: 60-100 bpm
  • Respirations: 12-20 breaths/min
  • Blood Pressure: 120/80 mmHg
  • Temperature: 36.5-37.2°C
  • Oxygen Saturation: 94-100%
  • Blood Glucose Level: 4-8 mmol/L
  • Pupil Size and Reactivity: 2-4 mm, PEARL (pupils equal and reactive to light)
  • Capillary Refill Time: < 2-3 seconds

Patient Handover - SAMPLE:

  • Signs and Symptoms
  • Allergies
  • Medications
  • Past Medical History
  • Last Oral Intake - last ins and outs
  • Events Leading up to

Pain Assessment - OPQRST:

  • Onset/origin
  • Provoking factors
  • Quality: sharp, dull, throbbing
  • Region/radiating areas
  • Severity, 1-10
  • Time
