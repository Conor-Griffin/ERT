H2S
  • Very high, can cause respiratory paralysis
  • STEL=15ppm, IDLH=100ppm, lethal >300ppm. Personal alarm goes off at 10ppm (TWA)
  • LEL=4.3%, UEL=45%
  • Can start to smell it at very low ppm (0.0005-0.3ppm)
  • Rotten eggs, but olfactory paralysis occurs at 100-150ppm so you can no longer smell it
  • 1.19 – slightly heavier than air
  • can accumulate in low areas
  • 3400 – pre-reduction
  • 3500 – sulphide prep
  • 3600 – sulphide leaching
  • 3300 (from NBL line if insufficient H202)
SO2
  • Moderate, strong irritant of eyes, nose, throat, lungs
  • TWA=2PPM, STEL=5ppm, IDLH=100ppm
  • Non-flammable
  • Sharp, choking, burnt-match / acidic
  • 2.26 – heavier than air
  • collects in pits, low lying areas
  • 4100 - Acid plant stack
  • 5700 – vent flare
  • Sulfur pit fires
NH3
  • Moderate, strong irritant of eyes, nose, throat, lungs
  • TWA=25ppm, STEL=35ppm, IDLH=300ppm
  • LEL=15%, UEL=28%
  • Pungent, sharp, irritating
  • detectable at 5–20 ppm
  • 0.59 – lighter than air, disperses upwards
  • can accumulate under roofs, enclosed spaces if airflow is poor
  • 3700 – Nickel reduction
  • 5900 – AMSUL plant
  • 3600 – sulphide leaching
  • 5200 – anhydrous ammonia storage
  • 3900 – pH control
  • Released from high pH solutions rather than flares
